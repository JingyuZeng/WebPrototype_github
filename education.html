What is the education for Diabetes?

Living with diabetes can be challenging. But you don’t have to deal with diabetes alone. If managing you diabetes day to day all gets to handled that’s when our Education service can help you learn more related symptom and methods. When you live with diabetes there are times when you might struggle with the physical diseases and the periods of depression, stress or anxiety. The day to day challenges can sometimes feel overwhelming, so it’s important to look after your physical health and emotional wellbeing. Diabetes can be easier to manage with the right support which you can enjoy everywhere and everytime, that’s why Diabetes NSW & ACT offers a Education service.

If you feel the need for some extra support just ring our contact centre on 1300 342 238 and ask to make an appointment to speak with our Psychologist Katherine Dixon. Please note that this is not an emergency service. Remember you don’t have to deal with your diabetes alone. Diabetes NSW & ACT is here to help you every step of the way.

Mental

When you live with diabetes there are times when you might struggle with periods of depression, stress or anxiety.

Feel free to have a confidential discussion over the phone with our professional psychologist which can help you get back on track to a happier, more fulfilling life.

Solutions related to issues:

Feeling tired, lethargic or irritable

Choose the way you like to relieve stress, like exercising, reading, hiking, etc. Staying in a good is helpful to your situation.

Strong Headache

Get enough sleep and stay in good mood. Then you should make an appointment to see your doctor immediately. This is one of the most serious symptoms, but donot feel too stressful and find us for seeking professional help.

Excessive thirst and drinking a lot of fluids

Eat fewer foods with high sugar and saltiness,implementing some fruits rich in vitamins instead.

Unexplained weight loss

Managing your weight can be an important part of living well and reducing the risk of complications when living with any type of diabetes; type 1, type 2, gestational or pre-diabetes.
If you’re overweight, the best way to lose weight (and keep it off) is through eating healthy, balanced meals with sensible portion sizes. It also helps to be physically active every day.
Healthy eating for diabetes includes selecting high fibre, lower Glycaemic Index (GI) carbohydrate foods and reducing fat, especially saturated fat. You’ll need to balance your food intake with exercise to maintain a healthy weight.

Skin infections or itching

Moisturize your skin that always choose a moisturizer free of additives, fragrances and perfumes. See the doctor ASAP.

Blurred vision

Blurred vision is a common sign of diabetes that isn't under control.


Good blood sugar control and regular eye exams are the best things you can do to prevent or control eye changes from diabetes.


If you were recently diagnosed with type 2 diabetes, then schedule an eye exam as soon as possible. If your exam shows no damage, then get a retinal eye exam every two years.

Mood swings frequently

Have a discussion over the phone with our professionally trained Psychologist can help you work through any stress or anxiety. You can access the help you need to implement strategies and get back on track to a happier, more fulfilling life.
